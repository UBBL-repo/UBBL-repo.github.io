Instrumentation & Control
Our research program integrates physics-based computational modeling, quantitative imaging across multiple scales, and advanced instrumentation to support the discovery and clinical translation of next-generation precise, personalized, and noninvasive medical technologies. We leverage ultrasonics and microbubble dynamics (acoustic cavitation) to engineer powerful feedback-loop systems for the noncontact control and imaging of living systems. This research has led to the engineering of novel targeted cell and gene delivery systems, cell-based diagnostic platforms, and image-guided interventions in the brain.
